How does LO handle shadow DOM? ▼
Lucky Orange traverses open shadow roots using shadowRoot.childNodes during the tree snapshot and subscribes to mutations within them. Closed shadow roots are inaccessible by design and appear as empty custom elements in recordings.
What happens to cross-origin iframes? ▼
Cross-origin iframes cannot be accessed due to browser security restrictions. LO neutralizes the src in recordings to prevent the player from loading third-party URLs. With lo-sensitive, the iframe is replaced with a placeholder screen via srcdoc.
How is canvas content handled? ▼
Canvas pixel data is not captured — only the <canvas> element itself is recorded in the DOM snapshot. The player will show a blank canvas, which is expected and intentional.
What does lo-sensitive do to images? ▼
Images inside a lo-sensitive parent have their src replaced with a 1×1 transparent GIF (BLANK_IMG) in the recording data, so no visual content from the image is transmitted to the server.
What does lo-sensitive do to iframes? ▼
Sensitive iframes have src set to about:blank and srcdoc injected with a styled placeholder screen. Because srcdoc takes precedence over src in the browser, the player renders "Third-party iframe — content not captured" instead of a blank white box. Implemented in fingerprint/src/tree-mirror/privacy.js.
How does scroll tracking work? ▼
LO reads document.scrollingElement.scrollTop to capture the current scroll position. If the page uses a custom scroll container instead of the document body, LO will not detect that scroll. Pages that need scroll captured must ensure their main scroll is on the document (i.e. body or html), not a fixed-height overflow div.
